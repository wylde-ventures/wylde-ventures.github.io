Rocketships are Rad

October 24th 2014 Jeremiah

My name is Jeremiah. I'm a current student of the Anake program class of 2015. I’d like to introduce myself and tell you the story about how I ended up here. However, when I tell that story it no longer makes sense because here is not where I thought it was. Strangely, it's where I wanted it to be, without knowing. I'm really not sure how I ended up here, but with great fortune it's exactly where I need to be. Clear as the fresh spring waters of the Cascades? No doubt.

This year is shaping up to be fairly radical. As such, I’m feeling drawn to share some of this experience with you (who are you? I do not know). But before I really get into sharing some of the experiences of this year (in subsequent posts), I want to give a bit of an overview of what the Anake program is from my perspective.

We've finished up just about two months of class. And as you can undoubtedly imagine I'm now qualified to tell you exactly what this program is about (this is not true). What they don't tell you about Anake (or, more accurately, what they didn't tell me, since clearly I'm about to tell you) is that the best possible description of it is that it is a ride on an interstellar rocket-missile cruising through space at warp speed.

The first thing you do is blast off.

The second thing you do is say "holy-mole I'm on a rocket ship, this is so cool, I've always wanted to be on a rocket ship, rocket ships are rad."

The third thing you do is say "Actually, I don't like rocket ships, I’m getting motion sickness, the interstellar is so far away, we aren’t going anywhere, space is just way too big, where did I put my wormhole device?"

The fourth thing you do is say "rocketships are rad..."

The fifth thing you do is say "wait a minute? I'm on a rocket ship in the middle of space? No bueno. What if this thing malfunctions? We could all die or start eating each other."

The sixth thing you do is say "the interstellar is rad..."

Then you wake up and realize it was all a dream. It's a school day so you head up to Linne Doran for class. You're clan is clan of the day so you get to work together to bust a friction fire and then help gather everyone for a fun day in the woods. After bringing the circle of Anakes together with a song you pass it off to the instructors so they can start up the lesson for the day.

Marcus looks into the fire for a while and then introduces the day's activity: "Everyone take a deep breath...this morning we'll be building an interstellar rocket-missile out of cedar logs and sword ferns.”

I'm a current student of the Wilderness Awareness School Anake program class of 2015.
I’m feeling drawn to share some of this experience with you (who are you? I do not know).
So read on, friend, read on.
